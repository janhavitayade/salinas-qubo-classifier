01 Band selection: 224 → 20

Each configuration selects its bands from a Fisher-discriminant-scored candidate pool. Pools spanning both the visible-NIR and SWIR regions (annealing, IBM hardware) pick diverse, non-redundant bands. Pools restricted to SWIR-only (statevector runs) get stuck selecting one consecutive, redundant block — regardless of qubit count.

04 What actually moved the needle

FINDING 01

Band diversity > qubit count

16-qubit and 20-qubit statevector QAOA — same algorithm, same candidate-pool region — landed on the identical band selection and identical 89.19% accuracy. More qubits without a broader candidate pool bought nothing.

FINDING 02

Spatial features: the single largest gain

Adding 3×3 neighborhood mean/std to 20 quantum-selected bands lifted OA from 91.46% to 94.85% (+3.39pp) on the same IBM hardware — the biggest jump of any change tested.

FINDING 03

Real QPU ≈ classical optimum, much faster

IBM hardware hit a QUBO cost within 0.12% of the simulated-annealing optimum, despite real gate noise — in 0.3 minutes versus 47 minutes for 20-qubit statevector simulation.
